All West

All West, which is an event put on by the West Tennesee School Band and Orchestra Association (WTSBOA), is a huge honor to be a part of. This honor band encompasses all of West Tennessee, and only the best players get to be a part of this experience, with each player required to prepare the given audition music, which can take months to perfect. Adde on to the audition music, each play must be able to play all 12 major scales on their instrument, and be able to perform sight reading, or playing a piece pf music without ever having seen it. Once a student is accepted for All West, they will attend the All West convention, which is a weekend event where they meet with the other players who were accepted, and have a few days of rehearsal together, and then perform as a group. This past year at Madison, we had 6 Madison students make All West for band. The students for the 2017-2018 school year that attendeed All West were Senior Maria Salsetto (clarinet), Juniors Morgan Collier (flute) and Ismael Silvar (trumpet), Sophmore Jesse Veltman (trumpet), and Freshmen Amber Martin (clarinet) and Skyler Perkins (clarinet). Please congratulate these students for their accomplishment.

All State

All State is an honor band that is an even greater honor than All West. The top players from the All West bands, as well as the top players from the Middle Tennessee and East Tennessee honor bands, are accepted into All State. In reality, when a student auditions for All West, they are really auditioning both for All West and All State. This is an amazing achievement, as this honor is only given to the absolute best of the best in Tennessee. We had only 1 student from Madison make All State for the 2017-2018 school year, and he was Sophmore Jesse Veltman (trumpet) Please congratulate Jesse on this amazing achievement.

Concert Festival

Concert Festival is an event that happens in a local city in each district every year. Bands from the area travel to this Festival to play for a panel of judges and recieve a score. They are required to play 2 prepared pieces, with at least one being a march, followed by a sightreading piece. Bands are rated on a score of 1 to 5, with one being the best and 5 being the worst, in each category, ending up with an average of all the scores. For the 2017-2018 school year, the Madison Academic Band recieved a 1 in every single category, giving them a perfect score. The Madison Band was one of only 2 bands in West Tennessee to achieve this feat.

State Concert Festival

Every year, the TMEA invites the best bands from all of Tennessee to play at the State Concert Festival. For the past several years, our very own Madison Band has been invited to this event, however, they were not able to attend due to their budget. For the 2017-2018 school year however, the band finally pulled enough money together to get to the State Concert Festival and perform. This is a huge honor, and Madison is very proud of their band.

Jackson Symphony Youth Orchestra

The Jackson Symphony Youth Orchestra (JSYO) is a local organization in Jackson, Tennessee put on by the Jackson Symphony. This organization allows players who audition to play with other local players their age, as well as a concert in conjunction with the Jackson Symphony itself. The audition process requires a given piece of audition music, a piece chosen by the participant, a sightreading piece, and knowledge of all 12 major scales. This year, we have had 7 students from the Madison Band that were accepted into this organization.
